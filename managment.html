LSS ( Lean six Sigma)

There are many benefits that come with getting Six Sigma training. Learn about the different certification levels and organizations that offer this credential.

What is Lean Six Sigma?

Lean Six Sigma combines principles from Six Sigma and Lean methodologies. By focusing on customers' needs, it aims to reduce waste and continually improve processes.

Getting certified in Lean Six Sigma demonstrates foundational knowledge and problem-solving skills to employers.

What is Lean Six Sigma Certification?

Lean Six Sigma certifications validate your proficiency in the methodology and corresponding skills. They are often referred to as "Six Sigma certification," covering Lean principles as well.

Certification levels are divided into "belts," as outlined below:

Belt Levels

Six Sigma White Belt

Understands basics of Six Sigma, its history, structure, and roles.

Six Sigma Yellow Belt

Understands methodology, participates as a project team member, and develops process maps.

Six Sigma Green Belt certification

problem-solving skills, works on defined projects, and serves as a trained team member.

Six Sigma Black Belt

Thoroughly understands methodology, leads teams, and assigns roles and responsibilities.

Six Sigma Master Black Belt

Expert in methodology and team leadership, quickly identifies "non-value-added" activities.

Project Management

Whether you’re launching a million-dollar product for a large, multinational company or starting your very own homegrown business, effective management is integral to the success of any project.So, when you undertake a project that requires the completion of numerous steps and the coordination of multiple stakeholders, you want to make sure that you say what you mean.

What is project management?

Project management is the organization and planning of a project from start to finish. People involved use particular skills, techniques, and tools to deliver a finished project with value to others. For example, developing software to streamline business processes or coordinating relief efforts after natural disasters both require project management.
